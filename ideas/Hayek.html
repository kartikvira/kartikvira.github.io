Friedrich August von Hayek

8th May 1889 - 23rd March 1992
School:Austrian
Key works: The Use of Knowledge in Society (1945), Prices and Production (1939), The Road to Serfdom (1944)

Hayek made very significant contributions to theory about knowledge and the price system. The Use of Knowledge in Society argued that free markets were an irreplaceable way of transferring information about scarcity and demand through the economy, a role that is now seen as one of the key benefits of markets. He also developed Austrian Business Cycle Theory. Hayek taught at LSE and the Universities of Chicago, Freiburg and Salzburg.
