Business Cycles

The existence of, causes of, and cures for business cycles are one of the most controversial areas in economics. Initial thinking on the existence of business cycles stems from Say's Law. Jean-Baptiste Say argued that "supply creates its own demand"; the value of what people produce is equal to what they consume, so there can never be a situation where the total supply of products exceeds its demand. This implies that any recessions are caused by an incorrect level of production for particular goods, not for the entire economy.
